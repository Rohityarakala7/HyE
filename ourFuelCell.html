This technical documentation describes the SisTeam 100 Gen 5 fuel cell, which was specially developed for mobile applications in heavy goods transport. It is an emission-free, sustainable energy source and offers a powerful alternative to conventional combustion engines. The SisTeam 100 Gen 5 is optimised for use in trucks, buses and other vehicles in long-distance transport, where high and constant performance requirements are crucial.


With a peak power of 130 kW and a system power of 100 kW, the SisTeam 100 Gen 5 fulfils the requirements of modern heavy-duty vehicles, while its lifespan of 60,000 operating hours ensures long- term reliability. This fuel cell relies on hydrogen as its primary energy source and utilises the


hydrogen cycle to generate clean energy by reacting hydrogen (H2) with oxygen (O2), producing only water (H2O) as a by-product. This makes the fuel cell an environmentally friendly solution without harmful emissions. The stacks can be coupled so that a system with a total net output of up to 400 kW can be expanded. Driving profile analyses currently show the need for 1-2 stacks in one system.


The SisTeam 100 Gen 5 is based on proton exchange membrane fuel cell technology (PEMFC). This technology is characterised by its high power density and fast responsiveness, which makes it ideal for mobile applications. Compared to other fuel cell types such as solid oxide fuel cells (SOFC) or alkaline fuel cells (AFC), the PEMFC offers the advantage of a faster cold start and more flexible operation for different load requirements. The choice of fuel cell type influences efficiency, temperature requirements and operating conditions, with the PEMFC being ideal for mobile applications due to its efficiency and ability to operate at lower temperatures.


2. technical specifications

1. Power: 130 kW gross (stack power); 100kW net (system power)

2. Fuel: Hydrogen (H2)

3. Operating temperature: 60-80°C

4. Efficiency: 50-68%

5. Stack dimensions: 1200 mm x 800 mm x 600 mm

6. Weight: 130 kg


The SisTeam 100 Gen.5's service life of 60,000 hours is a benchmark for a fuel cell, especially for mobile applications. As a rule, the service life of PEM fuel cells for mobile applications is around 20,000 to 30,000 hours. Thanks to various developments in the product and manufacturing process, the service life has been increased to a guaranteed 60,000 hours.


The ISO 14687 is the international standard that specifies the hydrogen quality requirements for fuel cells. For mobile applications, such as the SisTeam 100 Gen5 fuel cell, the hydrogen must fulfil certain purity requirements in order not to shorten the service life of the fuel cell. The hydrogen quality according to ISO 14687 requires a very high purity, typically 99.97% or higher, to minimise impurities. The main requirements include:


Purity of hydrogen: At least 99.97% (Grade D according to ISO 14687)

2. Maximum permissible impurities:
3. CO (carbon monoxide): ≤ 0.2 ppm (parts per million)
4. CO2(carbon dioxide): ≤ 2 ppm
